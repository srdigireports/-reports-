01 · The Month in Focus
Executive Summary

A healthy portfolio moving through a channel transition

March 2026 closed with all-channel revenue up meaningfully across nearly every property in the portfolio — strong signals that demand, brand, and conversion fundamentals are intact. Proper Hotels delivered +31.52% MoM on all-user revenue, Santa Monica Proper posted +56.67% MoM, Hotel June (parent) was +51.29% MoM, and Shelborne finished +12.5% MoM with a record YoY story as the property matures.

On the organic layer, six of our eleven reporting entities grew organic revenue month-over-month — led by Proper Hotels (+33.92%), Santa Monica Proper (+48.89%), Austin Proper (+29.98%), SF Proper (+17.84%), and DTLA Proper (+10.07%). Hotel June Malibu also moved organic sessions in the right direction (+13.78% MoM). Where organic softened, it did so against a structural backdrop we are actively shaping — the broader hospitality category is in the middle of the AI-search transition, where users increasingly resolve intent inside LLMs and generative overviews before clicking through.

Our answer to that shift is already in-flight. AI visibility is moving the right direction on 5 of 8 tracked properties — Shelborne, DTLA Proper, HJWL, ABH, and APS all gained visibility MoM, with Shelborne's share of voice up +0.84pp and DTLA climbing +4.21pp. Schema, CRO, and LLM-surface optimization work done this quarter is beginning to register. We enter April with clear levers: extending Shelborne's CRO treatment across the Proper portfolio, expanding Peec.ai prompt tracking, and capturing traffic via the metasearch and AI/LLM channel groups now being wired into GTM.
